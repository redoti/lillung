Pneumonia

Critical Disease

Pneumonia is a serious and potentially life-threatening condition caused by an infection in the lungs. This infection can be triggered by bacteria, viruses, or fungi, leading to inflammation in the air sacs of the lungs. Symptoms of pneumonia include high fever, productive cough, difficulty breathing, and chest pain. The danger of pneumonia primarily lies in its potential to cause severe complications, especially in vulnerable groups such as infants, children, the elderly, and individuals with weakened immune systems. If not promptly treated, pneumonia can result in permanent lung damage and a high risk of organ failure. Therefore, early detection, appropriate treatment, and prevention of respiratory infections are key to reducing the risks associated with pneumonia.

Cases

According to UNICEF, Pneumonia causes the highest mortality among children due to infectious diseases, resulting in the deaths of more than 700,000 children under the age of five annually, approximately 2,000 fatalities daily. This includes around 190,000 newborns. Nearly all of these deaths are preventable. On a global scale, there are more than 1,400 pneumonia cases per 100,000 children annually, equating to one case for every 71 children. The highest incidence is observed in South Asia, with 2,500 cases per 100,000 children, and West and Central Africa, with 1,620 cases per 100,000 children.

Symptoms

Symptoms of Pediatric Pneumonia

Cough

Common pneumonia symptoms include a cough that may be accompanied by mucus or phlegm.

Fever

An increase in body temperature often occurs as the body's response to infection.

Sore throat

Inflammation in the respiratory tract, including the throat, leading to a feeling of pain.

Noisy or wheezing breath

Cause narrowing of the airways, may manifest as sounds or wheezing when the child breathes.

Nausea or vomiting

Gastrointestinal symptoms such as nausea or vomiting may occur.

Diarrhea

Some children with pneumonia may experience digestive disturbances like diarrhea.

Decreased appetite

Serious infections like pneumonia can lead to a decrease in appetite in children.

Crying more frequently

Poor health conditions, including pneumonia, can make a child more fussy or prone to crying.

Fatigue and difficulty concentrating

Fatigue and weakness affecting the child's energy levels and concentration.

If your child has similar symptoms, please visit the nearest hospital immediately.
